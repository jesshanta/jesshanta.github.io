About Me

I'm currently in my 4th year of Software Engineering at the University of Waterloo. I am not your stereotypical Software Engineer. I have the same drive to figure out problems as most engineers do and I get the thrill of finally getting your program to work. However, I am a minority in the tech field and I have felt the consequences of that. For this reason, I am incredibly passionate about decreasing the gender gap in the tech field. It is important for girls to be encouraged to program from a young age so they can hopefully reach their full potential and not quit when they see they are one of the few women. I am heavily involved in outreach programs at the University of Waterloo to spark the coding interest in young girls. Diversity is important, and I want to be part of the change.

I love coding and technology. I want to make a difference in the world, and I am fortunate to be in a field where that's actually possible. I'm mainly interested in Software in Education and Medicine. I want good education available to anyone in the world. Everyone should be able to learn if they want to. I believe that software can help a bunch of the issues in the Medical field, and I'd love to be a part of that.
